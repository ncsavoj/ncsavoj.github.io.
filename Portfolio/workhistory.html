My professional path, a short summary of my

Work History

Currently I am employed by Staff Electric Company as a 2nd year inside-wireman apprentice.
Daily tasks include installing fixtures and devices such as lights and electrical outlets,
mounting electrical panels, running conduit, pulling wire, and terminating wire properly into whatever
it is feeding

This work requires the ability to pay close attention to detail. Everything must be done
in a timely manner to meet deadlines. Most projects are done by a team of electricians which
has given me a lot of experience coordinating and communicating progress, goals, and a daily
plan of action with my fellow coworkers.

Some of the things I enjoy about this job are the opportunities to create. Every day at the
end of my shift I can visibly see my progress. Problem solving and the ability to think outside
of the box often come into play as well.

Prior to my apprenticeship I was employed by the State of Wisconsin Dept. of Corrections as a
Correctional Officer(CO).

Being a CO requires an individual to have a very acute sense of detail. You quickly pick up on the
ability to notice anything out of place. Inmates have a lot of time and experience when it comes to
hiding things from Officers. Cell searches are a very high risk game of cat and mouse constantly
testing your abilities.

Documentation and the ability to relay information to your fellow officers is also a big part of
working in corrections. Everything gets recorded, from the times daily activities occur to any inmate
activity that appears to be abnormal. If an inmate that is usually very active and outgoing does not
come out of his cell during dayroom(a time when inmates a free to hang out in the rec areas of the unit)
it gets recorded. Passing this information on to the oncoming shift is vital for the safety of everyone.

Shortly after leaving the USMC I got my EMT-B license. From there I took a job as an EMT-B
with Paratech Ambulance Services. I operated as part of a two or three man crew providing basic life
support medical interventions as well as transportation for the city of Milwaukee, WI. We worked
together with the Milwaukee Fire Dept. to respond to medical emergencies.

Working as an EMT brings you very close to your coworkers. The joke in the field is that the
closer resembles a marriage than anything else. This profession will really teach you what it means
to work as a team.

The situations you are presented with are endlessly variable. You need to be able to analyze each
patients symptoms quickly and decide on a course of treatment. A good team will have a clear chain
of command and very rapidly agree upon a course of action.
